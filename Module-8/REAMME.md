# Module Eight Reflection

## Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements.

The client for this project was The Gaming Room, a company that developed the game Draw It or Lose It. The company wanted to expand its existing Android-based game into a web-based application capable of supporting multiple teams and players across different operating platforms. 
The software needed to ensure unique game, team, and player names while providing scalability, security, and reliability.

## What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?

I believe I did particularly well in evaluating multiple operating platforms and comparing their strengths and weaknesses. 
I analyzed Linux, Windows, cloud-based environments, and distributed systems while considering factors such as scalability, security, memory management, and long-term maintainability. My background as an automation engineer helped me focus on system reliability and performance.

## What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?

The design document helped establish requirements, constraints, and architectural decisions before implementation began. 
This structured approach reduced risk and provided a clear roadmap for development. Similar to planning an automation project before programming equipment, the design process helped identify potential issues early and improved communication among stakeholders.

## If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?

If I were to revise one section, I would expand the discussion of cloud-native technologies such as microservices, containerization, and serverless computing. 
These technologies continue to play an important role in modern software architecture and would strengthen the recommendations provided to the client.

## How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?

I interpreted the user's needs by reviewing the business requirements and identifying both functional and non-functional requirements. 
I focused on creating a design that supported scalability, security, reliability, and future growth. Considering user needs is critical because successful software must solve the user's problem while providing a positive user experience.

## How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?

My approach focused on understanding requirements, identifying constraints, evaluating technologies, and selecting the architecture that best met the client's goals. 
In future projects, I will continue using structured design documentation, requirements analysis, UML modeling, and architectural evaluations to ensure software solutions are scalable, secure, and maintainable.
